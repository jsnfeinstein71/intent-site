What Is Intent?

Intent is a fast local inbox for actions. It is designed to let you catch something quickly and decide what to do with it later.

That may be a thought, a to-do, a reminder, or a command. The point is speed, clarity, and minimal friction.

Built for real use

Intent was built to solve a practical problem: capture fast, stay organized, and keep moving.

Why Intent Exists

I created Intent because I wanted a faster way to capture thoughts without dealing with folders, confirmation boxes, account friction, or cluttered workflows.

I wanted something that felt immediate: tap, speak, save, move on.

Intent is built around immediacy. The goal was simple: get something out of your head and into motion without unnecessary friction.

Core Product Qualities

Intent is deliberately simple. It is designed to be useful immediately while leaving room for deeper routing over time.

Current direction

Fast capture

Capture by voice or text with minimal friction so an idea becomes an action before it disappears.

Route later

Capture first. Decide what to do with it afterward through sharing, staging, notification workflows, or future routing paths.

Built-in forgiveness

Undo and simple handling patterns help speed feel safe rather than punishing.

Local-first practicality

Intent starts from a grounded philosophy: an action inbox should feel fast, dependable, and close to the user.

Practical action surface

The app is designed for reminders, errands, follow-ups, commands, and quick operational notes.

Expandable over time

Intent is a strong standalone product today and a foundation for deeper systems later.
